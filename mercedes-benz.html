MERCEDES-BENZ
POCKET MUSEUM


GENRE: Museum Game

PLATFORM: Mobile

ENGINE: Unity

TEAM: 24

ROLE: Game Designer

DURATION: 8 months, pending release

PreviousNextClose

Take a ride into the past of the automobile!

• Choose one of three historic figures as your guide in the Mercedes-Benz Museum.
• Complete exhibition tasks and build your own collection of cars.
• Play minigames and expand your collection.

Features

DISCOVER: Look deeper into the dazzling exhibitions of the Mercedes-Benz Museum. Choose one of three pioneering figures from the Mercedes-Benz history to be your guide in the museum.

COLLECT: Complete a set of tasks with the help of your guide to collect your most desired car from each exhibition.

PLAY: Play one of four fun minigames even after you have left the museum and continue expanding your collection with new cars and paints.

Context

The Mercedes-Benz Museum game project shows how I can design a game for a specific audience and setting in accordance with the needs of clients.

Mercedes-Benz Pocket Museum is in development as part of research and development project between the Mercedes-Benz Museum and Stuttgart Media University. I worked as a game designer on the project during and after my studies at Stuttgart Media University.

The scope of my tasks extended into every corner of the design of the game, including: 

• Co-designing the gameplay loop in a way to encourage exploration in the museum without the game taking the focus away from the exhibitions, 
• Iterating on the game according to the feedback and needs of the product owners,
• Creating a set of museum tasks for each exhibition, 
• Pitching and designing three replayable minigames, each featuring three difficulty levels, 
• Writing all character dialogue for three historic guide characters in accordance with their documented personalities, 
• Designing the tutorial system of the game, 
• Mocking up the user interface and defining its flow and functionality, 
• Pitching and designing the rewards, collections and unlocks systems.
