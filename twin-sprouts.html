TWIN SPROUTS

ITCH.IO

Pick up seeds and grow plants in a vibrant environment!

* Feed your plants with water and sun spells to help them grow.

* Play cooperatively with or competitively against a friend.

* Save your individual and combined team high scores on the leaderboard.

PreviousNextClose

GENRE: 3D Platformer

PLATFORM: PC, Mac

ENGINE: Unity

TEAM: 4

ROLE: Project Manager // Game Designer

DURATION: 10 weeks

Description

Twin Sprouts is a casually-approachable 3D platformer featuring a blend of cooperative and competitive gameplay for two players.

Your goal in Twin Sprouts is to nurture your shared garden with a friend. You can plant seeds and cast water and sun spells in balance to help your plants evolve from one stage to the next. As they grow, so does the joy they bring to your garden.

You can either play in concert with your fellow sprout or compete against each other and try to achieve a higher individual score. Both your individual and combined team scores will be saved at the end of the gameplay session. The more competent each of you were during the round, the higher your combined scores will be in the end; giving you a stronger presence on the game's leaderboard.

Key Features

MAJESTIC PLANTS: Grow your garden from seeds to fully-grown plants. The more plants you have, the more joyful your garden could be—but beware their needs and do not plant more than you can take care of!

MAGIC SPELLS: Channel your magic to summon a rain cloud or cast sunlight on your plants to help them grow steadily. Recharge your energy with magic orbs scattered in the environment.

LOCAL MULTIPLAYER: Play in split-screen with a friend. Save your scores both individually and on team leaderboards.

My Contribution

Twin Sprouts was developed by a team of 4 students at Cologne Game Lab as a semester project in a period of 10 weeks. My tasks on this project were:

* Designing and documenting gameplay features,
* Creating and optimizing the game level to accomodate cooperative and competitive mindsets,
* Organizing playtests and iterating accordingly on the level,
* Planning and monitoring the project, distributing tasks and handling presentations.
