L.A.M.E.


Engage in a local asymmetric multiplayer experience!

* As the attacker, play a first-person shooter on the shared screen.

* As the defender, build defenses on a mobile phone to stop the attacker.

* React to one another in local multiplayer and upgrade your abilities as you go.

L.A.M.E. (Local Asymmetric Multiplayer Experience) is an experimental mix of two genres of gaming for two players in one space.

The first player (attacker) plays an agility-driven first-person shooter on the shared PC or TV screen, whereas the second player (defender) spectates the gameplay of their opponent on the shared screen and use their mobile phone to manage resources and command drones in a tower defense-like real-time strategy setup.

In L.A.M.E., I mainly designed the FPS gameplay and its relation to the RTS gameplay. My tasks involved the design, documentation and tweaking of many standard FPS features such as projectile behavior, aim assist, enemy AI logic and more. Perhaps most interesting was the player's grapple ability and the creation of a vertical level map, which needed to be reconciled with the top down camera view of the defending player. The game is designed as a half-spectator experience for the player on the mobile phone, thus the vertical level was designed to withold some information from the defender and give them a good reason to observe the gameplay of the attacker on the shared screen. 

A major highlight of my work on L.A.M.E. is the progression system. Both players can earn XP and customize their gameplay experience in meaningful ways over the course of gameplay. If the defender has unlocked a melee burst for their drones, the attacker may want to upgrade their grapple ability to get away from them safely. The defender may in turn invest in a speed boost for the drones, helping them reach the attacker more easily. This creates an engaging difficulty curve that encourages players to explore different playstyles by developing their abilities in four different directions.

L.A.M.E. was developed in a short period of 10 weeks and I acted as the Product Owner in our Scrum approach, planning and prioritizing with value-driven methodology to maximize the output of the team's efforts.

GENRE: FPS & RTS

PLATFORM: PC, Android

ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4

TEAM: 6

ROLE: Product Owner & Game Designer

DURATION: 2,5 months
