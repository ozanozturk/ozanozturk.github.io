L.A.M.E.


Engage in a local asymmetric multiplayer experience!

* As the attacker, play a first-person shooter on the shared screen.

* As the defender, build defenses on a mobile phone to stop the attacker.

* React to one another in local multiplayer and upgrade your abilities as you go.

PreviousNextClose

GENRE: FPS & RTS

PLATFORM: PC, Android

ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4

TEAM: 6

ROLE: Product Owner // Game Designer

DURATION: 10 weeks

Description

L.A.M.E. (Local Asymmetric Multiplayer Experience) is an experimental mix of two genres of gaming for two players in one space.

The first player (attacker) plays an agility-driven first-person shooter on the shared TV or PC screen, using a gamepad or keyboard & mouse. The goal of the attacker is to destroy the central mainframe which is protected by the four generators scattered around in the level.

The second player (defender) spectates the gameplay of their opponent on the shared screen and uses their mobile phone to manage resources and command drones in a tower defense-like real-time strategy setup. The goal of the defender is to protect their base at all costs and defeat their opponent once and for all.

Both players can earn XP and customize their gameplay experience in meaningful ways over the course of gameplay. If the defender has unlocked a melee burst for their drones, the attacker may want to upgrade their grapple ability to get away from them safely. The defender may in turn invest in a speed boost for the drones, helping them reach the attacker more easily. This creates an engaging difficulty curve that encourages players to explore different playstyles by developing their abilities in four different directions.

Key Features

One Game, Two Genres: Play an FPS as the attacker and an RTS as the defender in local multiplayer. Compete against your friends with different goals.

Vertical Action: As the attacker, jump, grapple and explore a vertical level while fending off waves of enemy drones sent by the defender. As the defender, command the environment and manage your tactics from a top-down perspective.

Reactive Progression: Gain XP, level up and upgrade your capabilities in four unique directions over the course of a round of gameplay. React to the upgrade path of your opponent and explore different playstyles.

My Contribution

As one of two designers in the L.A.M.E. project team, I focused on the FPS gameplay. My tasks involved:

* Designing and balancing FPS gameplay and systems; including projectile behavior, aim assist, enemy AI logic and more,
* Designing all player abilities, the highlight being the intuitive grapple ability,
* Designing and balancing the game's progression system,
* Creating the game's vertical level map using nothing but primitive shapes, designing it to accommodate both FPS and RTS gameplay from different perspectives,
* Planning the project timeline around prioritized user stories.
