L.A.M.E.


L.A.M.E. is an experimental mix of first-person shooter (PC) and real-time strategy (mobile) gameplay in asymmetric local multiplayer.

GENRE: FPS & RTS
PLATFORM: PC, Android 
ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4  
TEAM: 6  
ROLE: Product Owner // Game Designer // Level Designer 
DURATION: 10 weeks, finished development  

PreviousNextClose

ONE GAME, TWO GENRES

Play a high mobility shooter as the attacker and a real-time strategy as the defender in local multiplayer. Compete against each other with different goals.

VERTICAL ACTION

As the attacker, sprint, jump and grapple while fending off waves of enemy drones in a vertical level map.

As the defender, command the environment and manage your tactics from a top-down perspective.

REACTIVE PROGRESSION

Level up and upgrade your abilities in different directions. React to the progression path of your opponent and explore different playstyles.

L.A.M.E. is an example of my structured approach to collaborative design and shows how I can craft new and unique experiences with familiar ingredients.

L.A.M.E. was developed from pitch to fully playable state in merely 10 weeks. As one of two designers in the L.A.M.E. project team, I was responsible for all aspects of the game's FPS half. My tasks involved:

• Designing and balancing FPS gameplay and systems; including projectile behavior, aim assist, enemy AI logic and more,
• Designing FPS player abilities, including the intuitive grapple ability, 
• Designing and balancing the game's reactive progression system featuring four skill trees for each player, 
• Creating the game's vertical level map using nothing but primitive shapes in Unreal Engine 4, designing it to accommodate both FPS and RTS gameplay from different perspectives, 
• Assessing workload vs. production capacity, prioritizing user stories and iterating on the game design scope accordingly. 

UTILIZING SELF-DETERMINATION THEORY TO ENGAGE PLAYERS

I referred to SDT to meet the following three needs while designing the FPS gameplay in L.A.M.E.:

Autonomy: You have the agency to improve your in-game traits as you like and develop your own playstyle.
Mastery: Your skills are tested progressively as your opponent unlocks new abilities and challenges you in new ways. The unique verticality of L.A.M.E.'s shooter gameplay also presents a high skill ceiling to reach.  
Relatedness: The game leverages shared gameplay space, enabling two competing players to interact with each other.

USING ANCHORS TO DESIGN COLLABORATIVELY

L.A.M.E. was a product of collaboration between two designers. The duality of the design process extended into the concept itself: A game for two players, combining two genres on two devices—a challenge unexplored by other games. 

As a game designer, I always try to adopt a structured approach to my work. The need for structural thinking becomes even more evident in a collaborative environment. With that in mind, one of the first things I did in this project was to research and list possible approaches to gameplay afforded by our chosen genres: FPS and RTS.

PreviousNextClose

Laying out all the gameplay possibilities across two genres was immensely helpful in reconciling two very different genres in one game. It was also invaluable in enabling me and my fellow designer to stand on the same page.

As the designer of the FPS systems, the pillars I worked with were "Vertical Movement" (chosen to deliver high mobility action) and "Auto-Targeting" (chosen to offset the difficulty provided by continuous mobility, making it easier to aim). The use of these higher level pillars as anchors can be observed in every aspect of my work on this project: Aim-assisted projectile and grapple behavior, vertical level map, 3D enemy AI navigation...

MAINTAINING FLOW VIA PROGRESSION

In-game progression systems are among the best ways to engage players. In L.A.M.E., both the attacker and defender can earn XP and customize their gameplay experience in meaningful ways.

I designed the progression system in L.A.M.E. to achieve and maintain the macro flow by enabling players to react to one another's playstyle and upgrade their abilities accordingly to challenge each other.

PreviousNextClose

If the defender has unlocked a melee burst for their drones, the attacker may want to upgrade their grapple ability to get away from them safely. The defender may in turn invest in a speed boost for the drones, helping them reach the attacker more easily. Players are thus encouraged to pay close attention to each other's playstyle and react accordingly by developing their abilities in different directions.
