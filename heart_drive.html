HEART_DRIVE


Prove your humanity as an android!

* Go on a date with an unassuming human stranger.

* Type and combine available words into sentences however you like.

* Convince your date of your humanity, and win their heart.

Description

In HEART_DRIVE, you will assume the role of an android sent out on a dinner date with a stranger to demonstrate your ability to blend into human society.

The game is played with only a keyboard and offers unparalleled agency by enabling you to build your own sentences in a conversation. The humorous narrative of the game unfolds as you type your responses: Whether to follow the objective of seducing your date or explore the consequences of exposure is up to you.

Key Features

ANDROID ON A DATE: You are the secret property of HEART_DRIVE Corp., sent out on a first date with a stranger to prove your ability to blend into the human society. Follow the directives of your admin or ignore them at your discretion. Discover the true reasons behind your creation.

CREATIVE TYPING: Use the available vocabulary build your responses as you like. Type your sentences correctly and fast enough to speak at a convincingly human pace and avoid awkward pauses.

CHOICES, CONSEQUENCES: Do you want to flatter your date or push their patience to limit? The choice is up to you—so are the consequences.

Design Highlights

BRIDGING GAMEPLAY & NARRATIVE

The first time I got a hold of a computer as an eight year old, my first instinct was to type what I wanted to do: “Watch Pokemon”. Typing was the most intuitive way to interact with what I thought was an intelligent machine. The designers of earliest narrative games must have felt the same way considering the prevalence of typing as a gameplay mechanic in the classics of 1970s.

It's no secret that games often struggle bridging gameplay and narrative. Adopting a minimalist approach and building the entire premise of HEART_DRIVE’s narrative around typing helped me overcome this common problem.

The protagonist of the game was chosen as an android because the action of typing feels most natural when you are helping a machine formulate a response. The rest of the concept, including first date setting, emerged around this strong marriage of gameplay and narrative in the core loop of the game.

CREATIVE CONSTRAINTS & REPLAYABILITY

Text adventures traditionally allow the player to type whatever they want and let the text parser filter out invalid commands. While the idea is powerful, this unlimited freedom is in reality governed by a very limited set of verbs. The illusion of freedom dissipates as soon as the game shuts your input down and leaves you disappointed for having mustered your creativity in a blank space, which is often a more taxing effort than working with constraints.

HEART_DRIVE embraces constraints in order to breed creativity and to gamify the action of typing. When it is time to speak, your android character generates a set of context-sensitive words that can be combined to form responses in any way you like. This limitation of vocabulary, coupled with the max character limit the android can process and the ticking timer before your date is tipped off bolsters creativity and brings excitement to gameplay.

With up to 120 combinations possible in a given situation, you will want to go back and explore different paths. As the sentences you could not think of on your previous run gets stuck in your head, you can manage to work better with the given constraints on your next attempt.
