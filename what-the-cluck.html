WHAT THE CLUCK


Visit Cluckington for a round of Chickenball!

* Compete against friends in 4v4 cross-platform multiplayer between PC and PS4.

* Tackle opponents to steal chickens, and pass them between teammates.

* Bring fertile chickens to your farm and toss enraged ones at your opponents.

PreviousNextClose

GENRE: Team Sports Game

PLATFORM: PC, PS4

ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4

TEAM: 30

ROLE: Lead Designer

DURATION: 6 months

Description

What the Cluck is a team sports game based on a heated conflict between two chicken-farming families of Cluckington.

In this 4v4 online multiplayer experience, your task is to bring newly-hatched fertile chickens from the central barn back to your farm. Chickens can be passed between players along the way, but you must handle them carefully—an enraged chicken will only wreak havoc in a farm.

What the Cluck was developed as a cross-platform multiplayer prototype at Stuttgart Media University. Unfortunately, we could not continue the development of the game due to the graduation and departure of many key team members—including myself—at the end of the semester it was developed in.

Despite this, I continued working on the game in the following summer months in order to improve my skills in Unreal Engine 4, familiarizing myself further with the engine's landscape editor and cinematic tools in particular.

Key Features

CHICK-FULL OF HUMOR: Visit Cluckington, a small town of chicken farmers, and enlist with one of two foul-mouthed rival families fond of their cock jokes.

CHICKENBALL: Grab a fertile chicken from the barn and deliver it to your farm. Watch out; your opponents may tackle you and steal your chicken, or intercept it mid-air while you are passing.

CHANGING GOALS: Chickens don't like being thrown around. When the chicken you are carrying is enraged, your best bet is to toss it into the rival farm.

My Contribution

As a lead designer on the What the Cluck project, I was responsible for many managerial tasks ranging from the distribution of tasks to moderation of team meetings within an unusually large game design team of 8 students. My tasks included:

* Leading the game design team, distributing workload between team members and keeping track of progress using JIRA,
* Overseeing the design process of three different multiplayer game modes, only one of which was chosen to be developed in the end,
* Collaborating with other designers on the game's setting, camera and user interface.
* Creating and iterating on five different maps to find the right fit for different game modes and player count,
* Creating the cinematic tutorial introduction for the final level map using the Unreal Engine 4's cinematic tools.
