WHAT THE CLUCK


Travel to Cluckington for a round of Chickenball!

Compete against friends in a 4v4 cross-platform multiplayer mode between PC and PS4.

Catch and pass chickens between teammates. Tackle opponents to steal their chicken.

Bring fertile chickens to your farm and toss enraged chickens at your opponent's.

As a lead designer on the What the Cluck project, I was responsible for many managerial tasks ranging from the distribution of tasks to moderation of team meetings within an unusually large game design team of 8 students.

The primary creative output of my time on What the Cluck were the 5 game maps, all of which were designed and built by myself.

Although the project could not be completed due to the graduation and departure of many key team members—including myself—at the end of the semester it was developed in, I continued working on the game in the following months in order to improve my skills in Unreal Engine 4, familiarizing myself further with the engine's landscape editor and cinematic tools in particular.

GENRE: Sports

PLATFORM: PC, PS4

ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4

TEAM: 30

ROLE: Lead Designer

DURATION: 6+ months

▼ GAME DESIGN TASKS

- Led the content design team, distributing tasks between team members and keeping track of their work on JIRA.

- Oversaw the designing process of three separate multiplayer game modes.

- Worked loosely on the setting, camera and user interface with other members of the design team.

▼ LEVEL DESIGN TASKS

- Designed and built five maps for different game modes.

- Iterated heavily on different map layouts to find the best fit for different game modes and player count.

- Created the cinematic tutorial introduction for level maps; including animated sequences, camera setup and user interface.
