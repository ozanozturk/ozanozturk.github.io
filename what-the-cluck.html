WHAT THE CLUCK


GENRE: Team Sports Game

PLATFORM: PC, PS4

ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4

TEAM: 30

ROLE: Lead Designer

DURATION: 6 months

PreviousNextClose

Visit Cluckington for a round of Chickenball!

• Bring newly-hatched chickens to your farm and toss engraged ones at your opponents.
• Tackle your rivals, grab their chicken, pass it to your friends.
• Team up and compete in 4v4 cross-platform multiplayer.

Features

CHICK-FULL OF HUMOR: Visit Cluckington, a small town of chicken farmers, and enlist with one of two foul-mouthed rival families fond of their cock jokes.

CHICKENBALL: Grab an chicken from a barn and carry it back to your farm. Watch out; your opponents may tackle you and steal your chicken.

CHANGING GOALS: Chickens don't like being thrown around. When the chicken you are carrying is enraged, your best bet is to toss it into the rival farm.

Context

What the Cluck is a testament to my ability to lead a team of designers and produce high fidelity results using Unreal Engine 4.

As a lead designer on the What the Cluck project, I was responsible for many managerial tasks in an unusually large game design team of 8 students. My tasks included:

• Leading the game design team, moderating design meetings, distributing design tasks and monitoring the project on JIRA,
• Collaborating with programming and art leads to prioritize game features,
• Mentoring other game designers on design documentation and the use of Unreal Engine 4,
• Overseeing the design process of three different multiplayer game modes, only one of which was chosen to be developed in the end,
• Collaborating with other designers on the game's setting, camera and user interface.
• Creating five different maps in their entirety from whiteboxing to post-processing, continuously iterating to find the right fit for different game modes and player count,
• Creating the cinematic tutorial introduction for the final level map using the Unreal Engine 4's cinematic tools.

What the Cluck started its development as a cross-platform multiplayer prototype at Stuttgart Media University. Unfortunately, we could not continue the development of the game due to the graduation and departure of many key team members—including myself—at the end of the semester it was developed in. Despite this, I used this project as an opportunity to familiarize myself further with the engine's landscape editor and cinematic tools.

A highlight of my level design ambition on this project is how all five level maps I designed are connected to one another, separated from each other for gameplay purposes but inhibiting the same world in the background.
