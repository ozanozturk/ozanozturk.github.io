BLOG 7: POST-MORTEM

PREVIOUS ENTRY
 PROJECT PAGE

In the week following the final project presentation, our team held a post-mortem meeting where we discussed what went well or could have gone better during our project in order to generate takeaways for our future projects.

Here comes the topics in our agenda and summaries of the discussions we had:

THINGS THAT WENT WELL

Ideation

Keeping our options open without getting married to our initial game concept and giving ourselves an extra day to sleep on it helped us immensely in evaluating and evolving our concept with feedback received from the CGL staff and students.

However, our first ideation meeting could only happen merely a day before the pitch presentation due to schedules of team members (and this had to be a meeting where everyone could attend), so we agreed that the more ideal would have been to have that initial meeting sooner. 

Gameplay Design

After we settled on our game concept in the first week, it was filtered well enough that we could already see the next steps very clearly.

Gameplay design was my responsibility in the team and I am glad to have managed to work faster than ever before in finalizing and documenting the core design without causing anyone else’s progress to be blocked by a lack of design. Doing so with UI mockups right from the beginning also helped not only in my design process but also to communicate the design more effectively with the other members of the team. 

Mentorship Sessions

During the project, we sought mentorship from 9 CGL staff members about every facet of the project and we often accompanied each other in these sessions.

Better yet, we managed to act successfully on the feedback we received and doing so led to considerable improvements in several areas of the project. 

Team Collaboration in the Second Half

After a slow and rocky start, our collaboration improved greatly after the intermediate presentation as we met and worked at CGL much more frequently than before. Seeing each other more often and not having to communicate with just text and voice helped us function much better as a team. On top of that, despite the rising pressure, it was fun to meet and work together. 

Working at CGL also gave us immediate access to our colleagues and professors for instant feedback, so it is clear that we should aim to do so as often as we can in the future. 

Intermediate and Final Presentations

Even though we did not discuss this last point at the post-mortem meeting (but in the aftermath of the final presentation), it bears mentioning here that we were very happy with how both of these presentations went. 

Both presentations were rehearsed several times (although a few more wouldn’t have hurt) and the final presentation in particular received very positive feedback. What set it apart was how all team members talked only in service of explaining the game rather than talking at length about their individual work. Thankfully, judging by audience reactions and how entertained they looked, it went better than any one of us had expected.
