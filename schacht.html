Run, dash, swing and jump! Race against time to complete challenging tracks.

* Play alone or with up to 3 friends in procedurally generated tracks.

* Participate in regular challenges to rise to the top of the leaderboards.

* Create and share your own levels with the in-game level editor.

GENRE: 3D Platformer

PLATFORM: PC, PS4

ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4

TEAM: 35

ROLE: Game Designer // Level Designer

DURATION: 12 months

Description

Schacht is fast-paced 3D platformer set in an interstellar battle zone, developed by a diverse group of students at Stuttgart Media University.

The unique selling point of Schacht is the infinite replayability offered by the procedurally-generated 3D levels. The game automatically combines level segments to generate daily, weekly and monthly tracks in which players can race against their friends.

Schacht was greenlit by the Steam community and released for PC under the publishing label of the university in 2016. Two years after its debut on Steam, the game launched on PlayStation 4 and continued receiving updates thanks to the tireless efforts of my colleagues at Lab132.

Key Features

VERSATILE ABILITIES: Sprint on the ground, dash in the air, run along the walls, slide on slopes, swing with your grapple and hack deadly traps. Make your way to the end faster than anyone else can.

PROCEDURAL CHALLENGES: Hone your skills in hand-built levels, or engage in procedurally-generated daily, weekly and monthly challenges to secure your place on the leaderboard. Schacht features over a billion level possiblities.

CHARACTER CUSTOMIZATION: Wings, skirts, mustache or baguettes... Dress your runner for any occasion.

My Contribution

Schacht was a game of many firsts for me: It was the first game I worked on from pre-production to release. It also marked the first time I worked in a large team of game developers and collaborated with other game designers, as well as getting acquainted with industry standard tools such as Unreal Engine 4, Git, Confluence and JIRA. My tasks in this project were:

* Designing and refining character abilities including dash and wallrun,
* Collaborating with the other designers on the game design wiki on Confluence,
* Attending weekly playtests and liaising between the game design and quality assurance teams,
* Adjusting my designs accordingly to the creative input from the lead designer,
* Creating the introductory tutorial level of the game in collaboration with the lead artist,
* Designing, building and adjusting over two dozen level segments to seamlessly connect in procedurally generated levels.
