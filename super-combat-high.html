SUPER COMBAT HIGH


Don your school uniform and punch your way through high school!

* Choose your character and participate in levels themed after school subjects.

* Team up with fellow students to overcome the deadly challenges prepared by the teachers.

* Face the teachers themselves in thrilling boss battles.

PreviousNextClose

GENRE: Beat 'Em Up

PLATFORM: PC

ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4

TEAM: 7

ROLE: Game Designer // Level Designer

DURATION: 6 months

Description

Super Combat High puts you in the shoes and uniform of a student trying to graduate from high school by achieving success in levels themed after school subjects; working with or against other students to overcome the deadly challenges prepared by the teachers.

Every level is a world of its own, related to the high school subject it is designed after. The Chemistry 101 level starts in a classroom and extends all the way to the dungeons of Ms. Garlick—a literal witch who spends her days brewing hazardous concoctions and blasting off the teenage vermin that make their way to her domain in search of school credits.

Key Features

HIGH SCHOOL ACTION: Super Combat High is a school like no other. The only way to pass a subject is to beat rival classmates in a gauntlet of deadly challenges prepared by the teachers.

CO-OP EDUCATION: High school is where friendships blossom. Play alongside a friend and kick & punch your way together to a passing grade.

GRADES MATTER: Your performance will be measured with a grade. Grab lecture notes from your fallen colleagues to heal back up and increase your final grade. Find hidden teacher's notes to boost your maximum health.

My Contribution

My responsibilities in this project covered game and level design as well as project management. Some of my tasks were:

* Creating and updating the game design wiki on Confluence,
* Designing a melee combat system featuring movesets and frame data for 3 character classes (only one of which was developed),
* Specifying the AI behavior to govern 4 different enemy types,
* Creating the Chemistry 101 level in its entirety, including cinematic and scripted events, using self-made and free assets to convey the humorous narrative of the game,
* Using JIRA to distribute tasks in the team.

An accomplishment of note on this project was the task of creating the game's level without a level artist. Realizing the humor of this unique game concept with a limited number of self-made and free assets was the greatest obstacle I overcame during the development of this upbeat game.
